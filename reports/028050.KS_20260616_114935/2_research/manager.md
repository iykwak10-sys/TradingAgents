## Rating: **Underweight**  

**Rationale:**  
After thoroughly testing both sides, the bear thesis is better supported by the weight of evidence. The bull’s case relies heavily on one quarter of cash-flow surge, a lagging 200-day moving average, and an EV/EBITDA framework that currently offers no margin of safety. The underlying trends—multi-year revenue decline, net income compression, declining ROE, and no confirmed order pipeline—paint a picture of a company that has not yet inflected. The stock’s 130% rally from its 52-week low has already priced in much of the “recovery” narrative; the risk from here is asymmetric to the downside until tangible operating momentum is proven.

---

## Key Findings from the Debate

### 1. Fundamentals: Trend Is Negative, Not Inflecting
- **Revenue:** FY2023 10.62T → FY2024 9.97T → FY2025 9.03T.  
- **Net Income:** 753B (2023) → 757B (2024) → 617B (2025). Trailing P/E is ~17.9x on deteriorating earnings.  
- **Q1 2026:** Revenue of 2.27T (+8% YoY) still sits well below 2023’s Q1 levels; net income annualizes to only ~622B, not a breakout.  
- **ROE:** Declined from ~21.7% (2023) to ~13.0% (2025) due to equity growth outpacing earnings—a sign of capital inefficiency, not compounding.  

### 2. Cash Pile: A Moat, but Not a Catalyst
- 3.56T won net cash (~32% of market cap) provides downside protection but does not create value if simply hoarded. No buyback, no special dividend, no M&A track record; the cash earns near-zero returns, inflating ROE optically while return on tangible capital is far lower.  

### 3. EV/EBITDA Valuation: No Margin of Safety
- Trailing EV/EBITDA ~8.3x (2025 EBITDA 907B), forward ~9.4x (800B estimate).  
- Even using the bull’s own “conservative” 7.5x forward multiple, fair value lands around **45,000 KRW**, roughly 13% below current price.  
- At a cycle-trough multiple of 6x, the downside extends to 38,000 KRW (−27%). The cash cushion is already embedded in these numbers.

### 4. Technicals: Dead-Cat Risk Remains High
- A 31% plunge from ATH in three weeks is a distribution pattern, not a healthy correction.  
- The 50-day SMA (~51,000) is acting as resistance, not support; the 200-day SMA is still rising but is a severely lagging indicator.  
- The volume surge on June 15 (342M shares) came off oversold levels and likely reflects short-covering, not institutional accumulation.  
- Reward-to-risk from 52,100: upside target 64,000 (+23%), realistic downside 45,000–48,000 (−8% to −13%), worst-case 38,000 (−27%). That’s not asymmetric—it’s uncompensated.

## Summary

The bull side presents a “glass-half-full” interpretation that leans far too heavily on a single quarter’s FCF spike and a cash balance that has been inert for years. The bear’s data-driven case—sustained earnings decay, margin pressure, no visible order pipeline, and a stock that has already doubled—warrants a cautious stance. **Underweight** captures that view: maintain some exposure to avoid missing a genuine turnaround, but protect capital from what is more likely a cyclical value trap.
