### Why Not Buy?
- **No concrete recovery catalyst:** The 2026 Q1 operating profit was only ₩64m on ₩1.73bn revenue. Net loss was ₩3.98bn. The business cannot cover interest and sustain itself at this revenue level.  
- **Revenue trajectory is alarming:** From ₩285.3bn to ₩33.1bn to a run-rate of ~₩70bn (if Q1 annualised). The “seasonal” argument is weak; Q1 2025 was ₩0.95bn, so the “82% YoY increase” is from a catastrophic base.  
- **AI link unverified:** The Bull’s crowning argument is speculative. Without a single contract disclosure or customer mention tying revenue to AI, this is hope, not evidence. Even if AI exposure exists, the macro backdrop (hawkish Fed, inflation) punishes speculative small‑caps.  
- **Technical ambiguity:** The golden cross is a lagging indicator; momentum (MACD) is still negative. Buying into a dead-cat bounce without fundamental confirmation is betting on hope.

### Why Not Sell Everything?
- **Net cash floor:** ₩41bn net cash represents ~11% of market cap (₩374.5bn). Even in a worst-case scenario, downside is partly cushioned.  
- **Optionality:** A genuine new contract or a return of revenue to >₩50bn per quarter would change the calculus. Premature full exit risks missing a sharp re‑rating if the turnaround materialises.  
- **Technicals not broken:** Price remains above the 200‑SMA (₩14,510). A drop below that would be a clear stop‑loss signal; above it, some hope lingers.

### Conclusion
The debate is won by the Bear on substance. The Bull’s optimism is built on a fragile interpretation of a single data point (tiny operating profit) and a tenuous macro link. While the company is not on the verge of bankruptcy thanks to its cash hoard, the investment lacks the clear catalysts needed to justify new money. The recent bounce is a **selling/trimming opportunity**, not a buying chance. Reduce exposure, keep only a small “lottery ticket” with a tight stop, and demand real evidence of a revenue revival before committing more capital.
